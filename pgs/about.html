About me


Academic Profile

Jonathan is a full-time lecturer at Teesside University, he has a primary research interest in depression, schizophrenia, psychosis and the interaction between perception and action. Alternative research interests include statistics (Conditional process modeling) and machine learning, programming and app development (Python, R, JavaScript, C#), motion tracking and neurophysiology.

Learning and Teaching Interests and Activities
Jonathan recently finished his PhD research which examined the effects of different motor simulation states on imitation, agency and prefrontal cerebral blood flow across mental health conditions.
Jonathan is currently transitioning from a PhD candidate and graduate tutor to a full-time lecturer within Teesside University Centre for Applied Psychological Science, Centre for Rehabilitation, School of Health and Life Sciences, regularly teaching across the the Sport Science. To date, the majority of Jonathan's teaching has been deliverd on the following undergraduate modules: Introduction to Physiology, Anatomy and Biomechanics (level 4), Sports Massage and Clinical Experience 1 (level 4), Advanced Clinical Assessment (level 5), Human Movement and Motor Control (level 5). Jonathan also engages with disseration supervision (level 6) and the delivery of content to postgraduate students on the neurophysiological bases of motor control (level 7).
